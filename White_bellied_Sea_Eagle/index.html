Sexual Dimorphism

  • Females are noticeably larger and heavier than males
  • Plumage between sexes is nearly identical
  • Behavioral differences mostly seen during nesting, with females more dominant in defense

Unique Features

  • Known for soaring effortlessly on outstretched wings with slow, deep wingbeats
  • Emits loud, goose-like honking calls, often heard in pairs during territory defense
  • Engages in kleptoparasitism (stealing food from other raptors like ospreys)
  • Cultural significance: revered in folklore as a spirit bird in some regions

Diet & Feeding Behavior

Apex predator and opportunistic feeder

Primary Food

  • Fish (main prey, snatched from water surface)
  • Waterbirds (ducks, herons)
  • Reptiles (turtles, snakes)

Secondary Food

  • Mammals (occasionally)
  • Carrion (dead animals, roadkill, discarded fish)

Feeding Behavior

  • Soars high while scanning waters below
  • Dives steeply and uses talons to snatch prey
  • Often harasses other birds to steal their catch

Conservation & Research

Least Concern status but faces local population declines

Conservation Concerns

  • Sensitive to habitat destruction and disturbance near nesting sites
  • Pollution and reduced prey availability impact survival
  • Protection of mangroves and wetlands is critical

Research Importance

  • Indicator of coastal and river ecosystem health
  • Apex predator role helps regulate fish and waterbird populations
  • Studied for breeding ecology, territorial behavior, and migration

IUCN Status: Least Concern

Despite overall stable populations, local declines require continued monitoring and habitat protection
