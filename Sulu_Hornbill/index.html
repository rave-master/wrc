Sulu Hornbill

Anthracoceros montani

Living Treasure of Tawi-Tawi - On the Brink of Extinction

Class: Aves
CRITICALLY ENDANGERED

URGENT: Fewer than 50 individuals remain

This species is on the verge of extinction and needs immediate conservation action.

"The Sulu Hornbill is a living treasure of the Philippines — its survival rests on saving the last forests of Tawi-Tawi."

Physical Characteristics

Medium-sized hornbill with striking casque and glossy black plumage

Body Structure

  • • Length: ~70 cm
  • • Plumage: black with white tail tip
  • • Beak: pale yellow, topped with a casque
  • • Wings: broad, adapted for canopy flight
  • • Eyes: red or brown, depending on age

Sexual Dimorphism

  • • Males: larger casque, slightly brighter bill
  • • Females: smaller and duller
  • • Both participate in breeding care

Unique Features

  • • Cavity-nesting with female sealed inside tree cavity
  • • Rare and localized, found only in Tawi-Tawi
  • • Strong, loud calls heard across forest patches

Habitat & Distribution

Endemic to the Sulu archipelago (Philippines)

Extremely restricted range

Geographic Range

  • • Basilan and Tawi-Tawi islands
  • • Now mainly confined to Tawi-Tawi

Habitat Types

  • 🌲 Lowland rainforests
  • 🌴 Secondary forests
  • 🌳 Nesting in large emergent trees

Diet & Feeding

Primary Food Sources

  • • Fruits, especially figs

Secondary Food Sources

  • • Insects
  • • Small vertebrates (frogs, lizards)

Feeding Behavior

  • • Forages in pairs or small groups
  • • Plays a crucial role in seed dispersal

Human Interaction

• Harmless to humans

• Avoids people due to extreme hunting pressure

⚠️ Critical Human-Driven Threats

  • • Severe deforestation in Sulu archipelago
  • • Hunting and capture for trade
  • • Extremely restricted range makes species fragile

Conservation & Research CRITICALLY ENDANGERED

Conservation Concerns

  • • Fewer than 50 mature individuals estimated
  • • Ongoing habitat destruction in Tawi-Tawi
  • • Species on the brink of extinction

Research Importance

  • • Represents one of the last remaining hornbill populations in Sulu
  • • Highlights urgent need for conservation in the region

IUCN Status: Critically Endangered

Immediate conservation action required to prevent extinction

🚨 EXTINCTION ALERT

With fewer than 50 individuals remaining, the Sulu Hornbill could disappear forever within our lifetime.

Every remaining forest in Tawi-Tawi is crucial for this species' survival. The time to act is NOW.
